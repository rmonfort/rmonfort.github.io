About Me

I’m a self-taught software developer with a love of both web and .NET technologies such as HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, C#, and ASP.NET MVC. I’ve graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in computer engineering technology. During my time at Wentworth I completed coursework in object oriented programming, which included C & C++. I also developed solid communication, analytical, and teamwork skills through various presentations and group work.

For our senior design project, we designed automatic window blinds. It was a device that opened and closed window blinds based on the temperature of the room and the amount of light outside. It used a single-board microcontroller in conjunction with a temperature sensor, a light sensor, and a rotary motor. Our team consisted of 3 members, in which I was tasked with code development. The source code was written in C++. We successfully built a fully functional device within the 3-month time frame.

While working for Tatara Systems, I developed various scripts using Tcl and Expect, to increase the efficiency of the work place. At Accenture, I helped develop numerous business process management software solutions while also familiarizing myself with some agile practices such as backlogs, user stories, and iterative and incremental development. At Harvard Graduate School of Education, I created and maintained web forms for various courses using HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript. At CABEM technologies, I developed and maintained web applications for clients using .NET technologies. At Satuit Technologies, I write efficient and robust code that promotes maintainability, reusability, performance, and scalability.

On my own time, I read technical books and view PluralSight courses in order to expand my knowledge of my personal areas of study. I leverage both my formal and informal training to provide software and hardware expertise.
